Overview

The Little Mermaid is a 1989 American animated musical fantasy romance film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation and released by Walt Disney Pictures. Loosely based on the 1837 Danish fairy tale of the same name by Hans Christian Andersen, the film tells the story of a mermaid princess named Ariel who dreams of becoming human.

Ariel is the youngest daughter of King Triton, the ruler of the underwater kingdom of Atlantica. She is fascinated by the human world and dreams of living on land. One day, Ariel saves a human prince named Eric from a shipwreck. She falls in love with him and makes a deal with the sea witch Ursula to become human.

In exchange for her voice, Ursula gives Ariel legs and the chance to win Eric's love. However, if Ariel does not win Eric's love within three days, she will become Ursula's prisoner.

Ariel must now navigate the human world and win Eric's love before time runs out. With the help of her friends Flounder and Sebastian, Ariel must find a way to break the deal with Ursula and become human permanently.

The Little Mermaid was a critical and commercial success, grossing over $843 million worldwide. It was the highest-grossing film of 1989 and the highest-grossing animated film of all time until it was surpassed by Beauty and the Beast in 1991. The film won two Academy Awards for Best Original Song ("Under the Sea") and Best Original Score.

The Little Mermaid has been praised for its animation, music, and story. It has been credited with helping to revive the Disney Renaissance and is considered one of the greatest animated films of all time. The film has spawned a franchise that includes sequels, television series, video games, and a Broadway musical.

The Little Mermaid is a timeless story about love, sacrifice, and the power of dreams. It is a film that has touched the hearts of millions of people around the world.
